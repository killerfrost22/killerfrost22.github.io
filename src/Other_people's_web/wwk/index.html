Global Sections of Toric Bundles and Polytopes

Leave a reply

This post is a brief explanation about global sections of toric bundles.

A toric bundle on a toric variety is just a torus-equivariant locally free sheaf. In 1989, Klyachko showed that it corresponds to compatible filtrations of decreasing vector spaces [1]. In 2003, Perling gave a general description of torsion free sheaves based on \(\sigma\)-families [2]. Note that in his thesis, the filtrations are increasing instead of decreasing.

It’s like people in tropical geometry can use either max or min for tropical addition, we can use either increasing or decreasing filtrations for toric bundles. I prefer using increasing filtrations mainly because of two reasons: (1) the way of drawing Young diagrams (2) the polytope defined by a line bundle agrees with the one mentioned in either [CLS] or Fulton’s toric book.

Prime Congruence in Semirings

Leave a reply

Let S be a semiring. A congruence I is a equivalence relation of \(S \times S\) that is a subsemiring. Basically, it is a subset that satisfies:
1, For \(f \in S\), \((f,f) \in I\).
2, \((f,g) \in I\) if and only if \((g,f) \in I\).
3, If \((f,g) \in I\) and \((g,h) \in I\), then \((f,h) \in I\).
4, If \((f,g) \in I\) and \((h,l) \in I\), then \((f+g,h+l) \in I\).
5, If \((f,g) \in I\) and \((h,l) \in I\), then \((fh,gl) \in I\).
