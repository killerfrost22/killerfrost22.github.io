Effective Equidistribution of nilflows.

Nilflow is an example of parabolic dynamics which exhibits polynomial divergence of orbits. It is a natural question to ask the speed (rate) of convergence in quantitative sense. For higher step case, the related result is not known yet except Quasi-abelian because higher step structures does not admit renormalization flow. Instead, we adopt a method called 'scaling method' introduced by G.Forni and L.Flaminio's work and estimate quantitative speed of the ergodic averages on a special type of nilmanifolds.

Limit theorem of higher rank actions on Heisenberg nilmanifolds.

Limit theorem of parabolic flows (such as translation flows, tiling, horocycle flow, and Heisenberg nilflows) was studied by constructing of finitely-additive measures, asymptotic to ergodic averages known as Bufetov functionals. My recent work is to construct Bufetov function for higher rank actions on Heisenberg manifold and it proves limit theorem of Birkhoff sums under a full measure set of Diophantine condition.
